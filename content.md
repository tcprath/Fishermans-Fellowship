# Fisherman's Fellowship — Website Copywriting & Content Guide

> **For Claude Code / developers:** This is the single source of truth for all copy on the
> Fisherman's Fellowship website. Before writing or generating any user-facing text
> (headlines, body copy, buttons, form labels, microcopy, alt text, meta descriptions,
> emails), consult this file. Match the **voice** (§4), reinforce at least one **messaging
> pillar** (§6), use the approved **vocabulary** (§7), and follow the **style mechanics**
> (§12). When in doubt, run the draft against the **Do's & Don'ts** (§13).

**Version:** 1.0 (Draft, for review)
**Status:** Page-level guidance (§10) is mapped to a standard ministry-site structure and
should be reconciled with the final approved layout. Open decisions are in §14.

---

## 1. How to Use This Guide

Every page, button, email, and headline should sound like one ministry with one heart,
no matter who (or what) is writing it.

When writing any copy, work in this order:

1. Confirm the **audience and page goal** (§3, §10).
2. Check the **voice and tone** (§4, §5).
3. Reinforce one or more **messaging pillars** (§6).
4. Validate against **Quick Do's & Don'ts** (§13) before shipping.

## 2. Ministry Snapshot

- **Who they are.** Fisherman's Fellowship is a Christian ministry that brings fishermen
  together to grow in faith, stand with one another, and share the love of Jesus — on the
  water and beyond. It is built on Christ's call to become "fishers of men," turning a
  shared love of fishing into a doorway for the Gospel.
- **Why they exist.** To share the love and truth of Jesus Christ while building strong,
  lasting spiritual relationships — reaching people in practical, personal, and relational
  ways, meeting them wherever they are on their journey.
- **Core conviction.** Spiritual growth happens best through connection — with God, with
  His Word, and with one another. The ministry weaves three things into one mission:
  - **Evangelism** — reaching those who don't yet know Christ.
  - **Discipleship** — helping believers grow and live faithfully.
  - **Mentorship** — walking alongside one another for the long haul.
- **What makes them distinct.** People don't come for a ministry first — they come for the
  water. Fisherman's Fellowship goes where fishermen already are (tournaments, retreats,
  the daily grind) and offers real brotherhood and a real Savior. **The fishing is the
  on-ramp; Christ is the destination.**

## 3. Who We're Writing For

Talk to a real person, not "everyone." Picture the reader before writing the line.

| Reader | Where they are | What they need to hear |
|---|---|---|
| **The Christian fisherman** | Loves the water and the Lord, but rarely finds both in the same room. | "You've found your people. Faith and fishing belong together — here's your fellowship." |
| **The traveling tournament angler** | On the road, off his home church, hungry for community and the Word. | "We'll meet you at the tournament. Worship, prayer, and brothers — wherever the season takes you." |
| **The seeker** | Curious about faith, maybe invited by a buddy. Not "churchy." | "Come as you are. Start with the fishing; meet the One who calls you by name." |
| **The believer who wants to grow** | Saved, but stalled. Wants discipleship and daily habits. | "We'll help you go deeper — in the Word every morning and mentored along the way." |
| **The partner / supporter** | Believes in the mission; can give, pray, or serve. | "Your partnership puts the Gospel on the water. Here's the difference it makes." |

> **To confirm:** "About Us" reads as a brotherhood for men; "Who We Are" reads broader.
> Confirm whether the primary audience is men/fishermen specifically or all believers
> (see §14).

## 4. Brand Voice

**The voice in one line: _Brotherhood on the water._**

It sounds like a seasoned fishing partner who is also a brother in Christ — bold and warm
at the same time. Fired up about the catch and the Kingdom. Direct, encouraging,
unpretentious, and always pointing back to Jesus.

The source material moves between two registers: the bold, adventurous "warrior" energy of
*About Us*, and the warm, relational tone of *Who We Are*. Both are real. The voice holds
them together: strong enough to stir a man to action, warm enough to welcome a stranger.

### Four voice attributes

| Attribute | Sounds like | Not like |
|---|---|---|
| **Bold & direct** | Short, muscular sentences. Active voice. "You weren't made to fish alone." | Hedging, corporate filler, long wind-ups, passive voice. |
| **Warm & brotherly** | Speaks to "you" and "we." Invitational. "Pull up a seat. You belong here." | Institutional, distant, preachy, talking down. |
| **Rooted in Scripture** | Anchored in the Word and the call to be fishers of men. Lets the Gospel lead. | Vague spirituality, self-help, Scripture as decoration. |
| **Adventurous & outdoors** | Draws naturally on water, creation, and the chase — in service of faith. | Forced puns, gimmicks, metaphors that bury the point. |

### Using the "warrior" language well

The warrior/army/battle imagery from *About Us* is genuine, intentional, and biblically
rooted (the armor of God, the good fight of faith). Use it — but with intention:

- **Keep it tethered to Christ.** The fight is spiritual; the victory is His. Encourage
  faithfulness and courage, never aggression.
- **Balance intensity with welcome.** A seeker shouldn't feel he must already be a
  "warrior" to belong. Pair the rally cry with an open door.
- **Don't over-pour it.** One strong battle image lands; five in a paragraph becomes a
  costume. Let it punctuate, not dominate.

## 5. Tone by Context

The voice never changes; the tone flexes to fit the moment.

| Context | Dial toward | Example feel |
|---|---|---|
| Home / hero | Bold, stirring, invitational | A rally cry that opens a door. |
| About / Our Heart | Warm, sincere, conviction | A brother telling you why this matters. |
| Ministry pages | Clear, practical, encouraging | Here's what it is and how to take the next step. |
| Devotional content | Pastoral, gentle, Scripture-led | A quiet word over morning coffee. |
| Retreats / events | Vivid, inviting, restful | Step away, breathe, meet the Lord. |
| Giving / partner | Grateful, honest, vision-driven | Your support puts the Gospel on the water. |
| Forms / buttons / errors | Plain, friendly, human | Helpful, never robotic. |

## 6. Messaging Pillars

Every piece of copy should reinforce at least one of these five themes. Say them often, in
fresh words.

1. **Fishers of Men** — the founding call (Matthew 4:19). Everything traces back to it:
   the name, the metaphor, the mission. We're called to catch people for the Kingdom.
2. **Fellowship & Brotherhood** — you weren't made to fight alone. We carry one another
   through a world at war and stand together as equals for the Kingdom of God.
3. **Encountering God in Creation** — the water and the outdoors are where many of us meet
   the Lord most clearly. We use the beauty of His creation to draw near to Him.
4. **Grow & Be Equipped** — growth happens through connection: daily time in the Word,
   discipleship, and mentorship for living faithfully.
5. **Reach the Lost** — we go where fishermen already are and share the Gospel with those
   who don't yet know Christ — practically, personally, relationally.

## 7. Signature Language & Vocabulary

**The two-metaphor system.** This brand runs on a shared "language of love and fishing."
Lean on the natural overlap between life on the water and life in Christ — casting, the
catch, the line, deep water, the early morning, the long haul. Use warrior/army imagery as
a second, sparing layer (see §4).

### Words & phrases to lean on
- fellowship · brother / brothers · the water · fishers of men · the catch · cast deeper
- the Kingdom · the Word · walk with Christ · grow / go deeper · equip · stand together
- come as you are · the call · created on purpose · meet you where you are

### Words & phrases to avoid
- **Insider jargon without a doorway** — unexplained terms that leave a seeker outside.
- **Guilt or fear-based pressure** — invite and encourage; never shame.
- **Corporate / sterile language** — "leverage," "solutions," "engagement," "onboarding."
- **Tired clichés** — "unleash your potential," "next level," "game-changer."
- **Overstuffed fishing puns** — a little goes a long way; never at the expense of clarity
  or reverence.

## 9. Writing About Scripture & Faith

- **One primary translation.** Choose a single Bible translation for consistency (common
  ministry choices: ESV, NKJV, CSB, NIV) and use it everywhere. Confirm with client (§14).
- **Citation format.** `Book Chapter:Verse` with translation on first/important use —
  e.g., `Matthew 4:19 (ESV)`.
- **Use Scripture purposefully.** One well-placed verse beats five stacked together. Always
  tie the verse to the point it's making.
- **Lead with the Gospel, not guilt.** Truth delivered with grace; the aim is to draw
  people toward Christ.
- **Be theologically careful.** Keep claims simple and biblically sound; avoid divisive
  denominational debates in website copy.

## 10. Page-by-Page Content Guidance

> Mapped to a standard ministry-site structure. Reconcile page names/order with the final
> approved layout; the goals, messages, and CTAs carry over regardless of exact navigation.

### Home
- **Goal:** Stop the scroll, state who this is for, move the reader to one clear next step.
- **Key message:** You weren't made to fish alone — faith, brotherhood, and the water
  belong together here.
- **Tone:** Bold, stirring, invitational. Hero line + tagline; preview ministries; strong CTA.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Join the Fellowship` / `Start the Daily Devotional`

### About (About Us · Who We Are · Our Heart)
- **Goal:** Tell the story and the heart so readers trust the ministry and see themselves in it.
- **Key message:** We exist to share Jesus and build lasting brotherhood — called to be
  fishers of men, carrying one another for the Kingdom.
- **Tone:** Warm, sincere, conviction-led. The deeper voice and heart live here.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Meet the Ministries` / `Get Connected`

### Ministries (overview)
- **Goal:** Orient the reader to the four ministries; route them to the right next step.
- **Key message:** Wherever you are on the journey, there's a way in.
- **Tone:** Clear and welcoming; a few lines per ministry, each linking to its own page.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Explore each ministry`

### Daily Devotional
- **Goal:** Convert visitors into daily readers; build a consistent habit in the Word.
- **Key message:** A free daily word to keep you in Scripture and growing — right where you are.
- **Tone:** Pastoral, gentle, encouraging.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Get the Daily Devotional (free)`

### Rise Up God's Way
- **Goal:** Invite readers into prayer, mentorship, and discipleship support.
- **Key message:** Biblical encouragement, personal discipleship, and practical guidance for
  living faithfully.
- **Tone:** Supportive, personal, steady.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Request prayer` / `Connect with a mentor`

### Weekend Retreats
- **Goal:** Inspire readers to register and step away to meet the Lord in His creation.
- **Key message:** Step away from the noise, into the beauty of God's creation, into
  spiritual renewal.
- **Tone:** Vivid, inviting, restful.
- **Suggested CTA:** `See upcoming retreats` / `Register`

### Tournament Fellowship Ministry
- **Goal:** Tell traveling fishermen we'll meet them on the road; invite supporters into the
  mission.
- **Key message:** Worship, teaching, prayer, and community for Christian fishermen on the
  tournament trail — and the Gospel for those who don't yet know Christ.
- **Tone:** Energetic, on-the-ground, mission-minded.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Find us at a tournament` / `Bring it to your event`

### Get Involved / Connect
- **Goal:** Turn interest into a relationship; capture the next step for every reader type.
- **Key message:** There's a place for you: join, serve, pray, or partner.
- **Tone:** Friendly, low-pressure, clear options.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Get Connected`

### Give / Partner
- **Goal:** Make giving easy; show donors the Kingdom impact of their support.
- **Key message:** Your partnership puts the Gospel on the water and brothers in the boat.
- **Tone:** Grateful, honest, vision-driven — never pushy.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Partner with us` / `Give today`

### Contact
- **Goal:** Make it simple to reach the ministry; reduce friction.
- **Key message:** We'd love to hear from you — reach out anytime.
- **Tone:** Plain, warm, human.
- **Suggested CTA:** `Send a message`

## 11. Calls to Action

Keep CTAs short, active, and specific. Lead with a verb. **One primary CTA per screen;**
everything else is secondary.

| Goal | Primary CTA | Softer / secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Build the daily habit | Get the Daily Devotional | Read today's word |
| Join the community | Join the Fellowship | Get connected |
| Attend a retreat | Register for a retreat | See upcoming dates |
| Tournament outreach | Find us at a tournament | Bring it to your event |
| Prayer / discipleship | Request prayer | Talk to a mentor |
| Support the mission | Partner with us | Give today |

> **Avoid:** "Click here," "Submit," "Learn more" (when something more specific fits), and
> stacking multiple competing CTAs in one section.

## 12. Style Mechanics

### Naming
- **Ministry name:** `Fisherman's Fellowship` — use consistently; never abbreviate to "FF"
  in body copy. (Confirm canonical spelling/possessive — §14.)
- **Program names in Title Case:** Daily Devotional, Rise Up God's Way, Weekend Retreats,
  Tournament Fellowship Ministry.

### Capitalization of the sacred
| Capitalize | Examples |
|---|---|
| Names / titles of God | God, Jesus, Christ, the Lord, the Holy Spirit, the Father |
| Scripture & Kingdom terms | the Word, Scripture, the Bible, the Gospel, the Kingdom (of God) |
| Deity pronouns (house style) | He, Him, His when referring to God — apply consistently (confirm §14) |

### Mechanics
- **Voice:** active; second person ("you") and first-person plural ("we"). Contractions are
  welcome — they keep it human.
- **Sentences:** short and varied. A one-line sentence for impact is a feature.
- **Numbers:** spell out one–nine; numerals for 10+ and all times/dates (e.g., "6 a.m.,"
  "June 14").
- **Punctuation:** smart quotes/apostrophes; one space after periods; em dashes for emphasis.
- **Headlines:** sentence case for warmth, or title case for formality — pick one per
  template and stay consistent.
- **Accessibility:** plain words over fancy ones; meaningful link text; alt text on images
  that carry meaning.

## 13. Quick Do's & Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Picture one real reader and talk to him. | Write to "everyone" in a vague, general voice. |
| Lead with Christ; let the fishing be the doorway. | Let metaphors or puns crowd out the Gospel. |
| Be bold AND warm — a rally cry with an open door. | Use intensity that makes a newcomer feel unwelcome. |
| Use one strong verse, tied to the point. | Stack Scripture as decoration. |
| Keep CTAs short, active, and singular per screen. | Stack competing buttons or use "Click here." |
| Sound like a brother who's been there. | Sound corporate, preachy, or distant. |
